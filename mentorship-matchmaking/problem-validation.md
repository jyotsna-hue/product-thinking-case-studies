# Problem Validation

## Objective

Validate whether students preparing for placements struggle to discover and connect with relevant alumni mentors.

## Method

An exploratory survey was conducted with **50 students preparing for placements**.

The survey focused on:

- Difficulty discovering alumni mentors
- Current outreach methods
- Key challenges in connecting with alumni
- Interest in a mentorship discovery platform

## Survey Results

### Difficulty Finding Alumni Mentors

| Response | Students | Percentage |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Very difficult | 21 | 42% |
| Somewhat difficult | 18 | 36% |
| Neutral | 7 | 14% |
| Easy | 4 | 8% |

### Current Outreach Methods

| Method | Students | Percentage |
|------|------|------|
| LinkedIn cold messages | 29 | 58% |
| Asking seniors/friends | 11 | 22% |
| Alumni groups | 7 | 14% |
| Do not reach out | 3 | 6% |

### Interest in a Mentorship Discovery Platform

| Response | Students | Percentage |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Definitely | 27 | 54% |
| Probably | 14 | 28% |
| Maybe | 7 | 14% |
| No | 2 | 4% |

## Key Observations

- Most students rely on **cold LinkedIn outreach**, which leads to inconsistent responses.
- Discovery of relevant alumni mentors is **unstructured and inefficient**.
- Students show strong interest in a **structured mentorship discovery platform**.

## Insight

The primary friction is **mentor discovery**, not willingness to seek mentorship.

Students are actively trying to connect with alumni but lack an efficient way to identify the right mentors.

## Conclusion

The survey results suggest a clear opportunity to design a product that simplifies alumni mentor discovery and improves mentorship access during placements.
