# Prototype

## Objective

Explore a simple interface that helps students quickly discover relevant alumni mentors during placements.

The goal of the prototype is to reduce the friction involved in discovering mentors and initiating conversations.

## Concept

The prototype explores a **swipe-based discovery interface**, similar to dating apps, where students can quickly browse potential mentors.

Instead of searching through alumni lists or LinkedIn, students can discover mentors through a guided interface.

## Core Flow

1. Student opens the mentorship platform
2. The platform displays mentor profiles
3. Students swipe through profiles based on relevance
4. When a student expresses interest, they can send a mentorship request

## Mentor Profile Information

Each mentor card would include:

- Name
- Company
- Role
- Years of experience
- Areas they can mentor in (interview prep, role insights, referrals)

## Why Swipe Discovery

A swipe interface allows:

- Fast exploration of mentors
- Reduced decision fatigue
- More engagement compared to static lists

Students can quickly filter mentors by relevance without navigating complex menus.

## Early Assumptions

- Students prefer quick discovery over manual search
- A lightweight interaction lowers the barrier to outreach
- Structured mentor profiles improve match quality

## Next Step

If this prototype shows promise, the next step would be testing it with students to evaluate:

- usability
- engagement
- mentor match relevance
