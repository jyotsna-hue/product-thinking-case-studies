# Problem Discovery

## Context

UPI has made digital payments extremely convenient.

While this convenience improves accessibility, it also reduces the psychological friction associated with spending money.

As a result, users often make frequent small-value payments without fully realizing how these transactions accumulate over time.

---

## Observation

Many users experience the following patterns:

- Multiple small transactions throughout the day
- Difficulty recalling where money was spent
- Realizing overspending only at the end of the week or month

Because digital payments happen instantly, users rarely pause to reflect before completing a transaction.

---

## Problem Statement

UPI users struggle to stay aware of their spending behavior because digital payments make transactions quick and frictionless.

As a result, many users accumulate small expenses without realizing their overall financial impact.

---

## Opportunity

There is an opportunity to introduce lightweight spending awareness mechanisms that encourage users to reflect briefly before completing a transaction.

Instead of restricting payments, the system should guide users through contextual insights and behavioral nudges.

---

## Initial Hypothesis

If users receive contextual prompts before completing certain payments, they may become more aware of their spending patterns and make more mindful financial decisions.
